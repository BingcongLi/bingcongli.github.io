About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at University of Minnesota working with Signal Processing in Networking and Communications (SPiNCOM) group under the supervision of Prof. Georgios B. Giannakis.

My current research interests span optimization, machine learning, signal processing, and their applications in mordern machine learning tasks such as distributionally robust learning and learning over graphs.

Below is a brief summary of topics I have done some work on:

  • Optimization: Efficient algorithms for training large neural networks; design and analsis for first-order (non)convex algorithms; stochastic optimization for large-scale problems.

  • Machine learning: Multi-armed (contextual) bandits with its application to recommender systems; and trustworthy machine learning.

  • Signal processing: Learning over graphs; resource allocation in IoT and smart grids.
