Optimization

The list below contains various faster and practical optimizers that can improve numerical efficiency.

  • Heavy Ball Momentum for Conditional Gradient
    B. Li, A. Sadeghi, and G. B. Giannakis
    Proc. NeurIPS 2021. (acceptance rate 26%)
    TLDR: Heavy ball momentum is helpful to obtain a tightened stopping criterion in Conditional Gradient (or FW) method.
    [poster] [code]

  • A Momentum-Guided Frank-Wolfe Algorithm
    B. Li, Mario Coutino, G. B. Giannakis, and G. Leus
    IEEE Transactions on Signal Processing, vol. 69, pp. 3597-3611, June 2021.
    TLDR: Nesterov's momentum improves convergence rate of FW on a class of problems.
    [code]

  • Enhancing Parameter-Free Frank Wolfe with an Extra Subproblem
    B. Li, L. Wang, G. B. Giannakis, and Z. Zhao
    Proc. AAAI 2021. (acceptance rate 18.7%)
    TLDR: A variant of Nesterov's momentum in FW further improves convergence rate in the TSP paper above.
    [poster] [code]

  • Almost Tune-Free Variance Reduction
    B. Li, L. Wang, and G. B. Giannakis
    Proc. ICML 2020. (acceptance rate 21.8%)
    TLDR: Practical SVRG and SARAH that eliminate manually tuning parameters.

  • Adaptive Step Sizes in Variance Reduction via Regularization
    B. Li, and G. B. Giannakis.
    Technical repoort.
    TLDR: An extension of the above ICML paper to more general problems.

  • On the Convergence of SARAH and Beyond
    B. Li, M. Ma, and G. B. Giannakis
    Proc. AISTATS 2020.
    TLDR: A variant of SARAH is developed with more rigorous convergence analysis.

  • Frank Wolfe with Averaged Gradients
    Y. Zhang, B. Li, and G. B. Giannakis
    Proc. ICASSP 2021.
    TLDR: A few variants of FW are developed.

  • Revisit of Estimate Sequence for Accelerated Gradient Method
    B. Li, M. Coutino, and G. B. Giannakis
    Proc. ICASSP 2020.
    TLDR: Deeper understaning and more insights for Nesterov's momentum.

Distributed systems

Works focusing on distributed training and systems are summarized here.

  • TWICE: Two-Way Innovation Compression for Communication Efficient Federated Learning
    J. Sun, B. Li, T. Chen, Q. Yang, Z. Yang, and G. B. Giannakis
    submitted to IEEE Transactions on Cybernetics, July 2020.
    TLDR: We propose a novel communication efficient method for federated learning.

  • Tight Linear Convergence Rate of ADMM for Decentralized Optimization
    M. Ma, B. Li, and G. B. Giannakis.
    Technical report.
    TLDR: Extension of an ADMM paper to more general communication graphs.

  • Real-time Optimal Energy Management with Reduced Battery Capacity Requirements
    B. Li, T. Chen, X. Wang, and G. B. Giannakis
    IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid, vol. 10, no. 2, pp. 1928-1938, March 2019.
    TLDR: A learning based method to reduce the battery capacity requirement in smart grids, and its distributed implementation via ADMM.

  • Real-Time Energy Management with Improved Cost-Capacity Tradeoff
    B. Li, T. Chen, X. Wang, and G. B. Giannakis
    Proc. GlobalSIP 2017.
    TLDR: Short version of the above TSG paper.

Learning under uncertainty

Here goes papers to harness statistical efficiency when learning under uncertainties.

  • Distributionally Robust Semi-supervised Learning over Graphs
    A. Sadeghi, M. Ma, B. Li, and G. B. Giannakis
    Proc. ICLR 2021, Workshop on Responsible AI.
    TLDR: We develop distributionally robust graph neural networks (GNN).

  • Adversarial Linear Contextual Bandits with Graph-Structured Side Observations
    L. Wang, B. Li, H. Zhou, G. B. Giannakis, L. R. Varshney, and Z. Zhao
    Proc. AAAI 2021. (acceptance rate 18.7%)
    TLDR: Two efficient algorithms are proposed for contextual bandits with side observations.
    [poster]

  • Bandit Online Learning with Unknown Delays
    B. Li, T. Chen, and G. B. Giannakis
    Proc. AISTATS 2019. (acceptance rate 32.4%)
    TLDR: We study adversarial bandit problem under delayed feedback, where the delay is unknown. This is useful for certain scenarios in recommender systems.
    [poster]

  • Near-Optimal Algorithms for Piecewise-Stationary Cascading Bandits
    L. Wang, H. Zhou, B. Li, L.R. Varshney, and Z. Zhao
    Proc. ICASSP 2021.
    TLDR: Cascading bandits in piecewise-stationary environment.

  • Secure Mobile Edge Computing in IoT via Collaborative Online Learning
    B. Li, T. Chen, and G. B. Giannakis
    IEEE Transactions on Signal Processing, vol. 67, no. 23, pp. 5922-5935, December 2019.
    TLDR: We handle jamming and adversarial attacks in edge computing using collaborative bandit algorithms.

  • Secure Edge Computing in IoT via Online Learning
    B. Li, T. Chen, and G. B. Giannakis
    Proc. Asilomar 2018.
    TLDR: A short version of the TSP paper above.

  • Finite-Time Analysis of Biased Stochastic Approximation with Application to Reinforcement Learning
    G. Wang, B. Li, and G. B. Giannakis
    submitted to IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control, March 2020.
    TLDR: This paper studies the convergence of stochastic approximation under Markovian environment for reinforcement learning.
