# About

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at ETH Zürich working with [Prof. Niao He](https://niao-he.com). I completed my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Minnesota, advised by [Prof. Georgios B. Giannakis](https://cse.umn.edu/ece/georgios-giannakis).

**Research interests.** Drawing on optimization, reinforcement learning, and signal processing, I develop tools to make foundation models more capable, efficient, and deployable. My research advances a **scaling view of intelligence** — where model performance is optimized jointly with compute, memory, data, and accessibility.

- **Scaling up** to push the limits of foundation models ||| **L** is for the large in **L**LMs. My research asks: how can we scale large models efficiently across training, fine-tuning, and test-time inference? I take a learning-dynamics-aware perspective to design scalable architectures that are as expressive as existing ones, but simpler and easier to train and reason with; see e.g., [Scaling deeper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.09009) [Scaling wider](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.03133) [Scaling at test time](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14853).
- **Scaling down** to enable on-device intelligence ||| We address the challenge of making increasingly capable yet massive models accessible in real-world settings. Our goal is to democratize LLM capabilities by enabling efficient deployment on resource-constrained and on-device platforms; see e.g., [On-device private fine-tuning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.09639) [Fluid deployment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00942).
- **Foundations** to understand the computational principles of deep learning ||| By uncovering mechanisms that are obscured by black-box views of LLMs, we develop foundational insights for making computation more efficient; see e.g., [Initialization for exponentially faster convergence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.18965) [Implicit regularization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.14802) [Weight normalization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.01175) [Edge of stability](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14669).

Some of these ideas are further discussed in my [research notes](https://bingcongli.github.io/bc.thoughts/).
